### Executive Summary

InfraFabric mirrors human cycles — manic acceleration, depressive reflection, dream synthesis, and reward homeostasis — as **governance patterns rather than pathologies**.

# PART IV: IF.armour PRODUCTION DEPLOYMENT

***Auditor's Note:*** *This Part documents `IF.armour`, a production-grade LLM defense-in-depth architecture. It was developed in response to a high-stakes request to research attack vectors safely. Its methodology—separating adversarial research from production mitigation—and its robust, auditable components make it a core pillar of the InfraFabric security posture.*

## Section 4.1: Introduction & Tri-Protect Architecture
`IF.armour` is a comprehensive, auditable, and deployable security framework designed to shield both the user and the LLM provider from harm. Its architecture is based on the **Tri-Protect Loop**: a three-ring defense model ensuring that raw adversarial data never contaminates the production environment.
1.  **Outer Ring (IF.research):** A secure, air-gapped vault for adversarial research.
2.  **Middle Ring (IF.mitigate):** The orchestration layer where sanitized, defensive artifacts are tested and approved.
3.  **Inner Ring (IF.guard):** The production LLM, which only ever consumes verified mitigations.

## Section 4.2: Two-Track Separation: Research Vault vs. Production
The cornerstone of `IF.armour` is the strict separation of the **Secure Research Vault** from the **Production Orchestrator**. The production LLM *never* directly ingests raw exploit corpora. Every artifact is assigned an immutable ID, timestamp, SHA-256 hash, and a signed manifest.

## Section 4.3: Immutable Logging & Audit Protocol
`IF.armour` mandates a tamper-evident logging protocol using a detailed JSON schema, an append-only (WORM) store, and a hash chain mirrored nightly to an external Hardware Security Module (HSM).

## Section 4.4: Operational Controls: Multisig & Canary Policy
Critical actions, such as deploying a mitigation, require a **2-of-3 signature approval** from distinct roles. All new mitigations are first rolled out to a **canary shard** (e.g., 1% of traffic) and shadow-monitored, with automated rollback on failure.

## Section 4.5: KPIs & Audit Evidence for Compliance
`IF.armour` is designed to be auditable. External auditors receive a verifiable evidence chain (signed manifests, canary metrics, RBAC logs) without exposing sensitive exploit data, allowing them to confirm that controls were in place and followed.
